01The integration reality

Three ways to touch LinkedIn programmatically. Only one fits what you described.

Closed to us

Official LinkedIn API

Restricted to approved partners (ads, recruiting, CRM sync). No automated connection requests or member messaging, period. Not an option for outreach.

Recommended

Account-connection API (Unipile)

Connects your real LinkedIn session and exposes clean API endpoints: send invitation, send message, read inbox, pull profiles. ~€49/mo. Sherlock stays the brain — audience, cadence, and logs are all ours. This is what vendors like Outbounder use under the hood.

Turnkey alternative

Full tool (HeyReach etc.)

Campaign UI, sequences, and an API on top. Faster to first send, but it's another vendor black box — the exact thing you're trying to get away from. Keep as fallback.

Honest caveat: every one of these — including Outbounder today — automates a real LinkedIn account outside LinkedIn's terms of service. The risk is managed with volume caps and warm-up (below), and Unipile enforces rate limits, but it is never zero. The account doing the sending carries the risk, so we decide deliberately whether that's your profile or a dedicated seat.

03The sequence

Minimal by design — three touches, then stop. Every step logged to a tracking Sheet (prospect, stage, dates, replies, meetings).

  1. Build & dedupe the list

    Sales Nav search → tracking Sheet. Remove existing connections, current partners, and anyone already in an Outbounder sequence so nobody gets hit twice.

  2. Connection request 20–25/day, ~100/wk

    Short personalized note or none at all — blank invites often accept better than salesy ones. We A/B this from week one.

  3. Message 1 — the opener within 24h of accept

    Thanks + one specific observation about their agency or a programmatic point. No pitch, no link.

  4. Message 2 — the substance day +3–4

    The MCG one-liner: upstream supply optimization, what it typically does for an agency's programmatic margin. One idea, three sentences.

  5. Message 3 — the ask, then silence day +8–10

    Direct: 20 minutes with you. If no reply, the sequence ends — no chaser #4. Unaccepted invites are withdrawn after 3 weeks to keep the pending count clean.

Any reply, at any step, stops the cadence instantly. I flag it to you on Dispatch with the thread and a suggested response — a human takes over the moment a conversation starts.

04Guardrails

Volume caps≤25 invites/day, ~100/week — LinkedIn's practical ceiling. Messages capped similarly.
Warm-up rampWeeks 1–2 at half volume before full pace. New automation on an account is the flag LinkedIn watches for.
Human rhythmSends only in US business hours, randomized minutes between actions — never batch-fired.
No identical templatesEach message varies per prospect. Identical copy across accounts is how LinkedIn correlates automation.
