01
Bid duplication

One impression. Four bids.

The same ad request — one real user, one impression opportunity — enters the open market through multiple SSPs and multiple accounts. By the time it reaches your DSP it has been duplicated four times over: same domain, same user, but each copy carries a different price and slightly different metadata. Your DSP can't tell it's bidding against itself.

MCG sees the full picture upstream and collapses the duplicates back into the single opportunity they always were — so you bid once, cleanly.

02
Deal mapping

Thousands of deal IDs. No map.

SSPs strike deals with publishers, and publishers mint deal IDs by the thousand — one for live sports, one for VOD, on and on. Each SSP describes the same inventory differently: name, format, and quality never line up. Matching that sprawl to what a buyer actually wants is a nightmare.

MCG normalizes every deal into one taxonomy and maps it straight to buyer intent — so a request for "live sports, premium" returns exactly that, across every SSP.

04
Bias & inventory shaping

SSPs play favorites.

SSPs prioritize one DSP over another. Priority-1 demand can see 2B requests while priority-2 demand sees only 750M of the same supply. If your DSP is on the wrong side of that, you simply never see the inventory.

Because MCG recognizes the bias, we can shape inventory — push more volume to where you need it and surface supply you weren't being shown.
